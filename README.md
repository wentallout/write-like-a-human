# 🧠 Write Like A Human (WLAH)

**The antidote to the "AI Register."**

Most AI text is caught not by specific words, but by a "signature of perfection." AI is optimized for **completeness, clarity, and structure**. Humans, however, are naturally **partial, biased, messy, and specific.**

This project provides the tools and protocols to move AI writing away from the "mean" and into authentic, human-territory.

---

## ⚖️ The Golden Equation

> **AI-Generated** = Meta-commentary + Generic Scaffolding + Absolute Completeness
> 
> **Human-Passing** = Physical Grounding + Operational Friction + Low-Probability Detail

---

## 🛠️ The `wlah` Skill

The `wlah` skill implements a **Human-Like Writing Algorithm** derived from linguistic field guides (including Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing"). It doesn't just "remove words"; it breaks the underlying behavioral patterns of large language models.

### How it breaks the AI Register:

1.  **Kills Meta-Commentary:** AI loves to explain *why* a paragraph is important. `wlah` strips the "marking a pivotal moment" filler and gets straight to the point.
2.  **Introduces Operational Friction:** Humans mention messy realities—broken equipment, data gaps, or personal biases. `wlah` injects these "frictions" to simulate lived experience.
3.  **Destroys Scaffolding:** It aggressively bans "Overall," "In conclusion," and "Furthermore," forcing natural, jumpy transitions.
4.  **Optimizes for Burstiness:** Most AI writes sentences of similar length. `wlah` forces high-volatility sentence patterns (a 40-word flow followed by a 4-word punch).
5.  **Grounds in Mechanism:** It replaces corporate abstractions ("fostering innovation") with physical mechanisms ("the soldering iron kept smoking").

## 🚀 Getting Started

To use the protocol, simply mention `@wlah` in your prompt or import the skill into your agentic workflow.

### Usage Patterns

**1. General Humanization**
> "Write a blog post about coffee using `@wlah`. Make it sound opinionated and slightly abrasive."

**2. Stylome Cloning**
> "Analyze my writing style in `samples/blog_post.md` and use the `@wlah` metrics to replicate my voice in this new draft."
